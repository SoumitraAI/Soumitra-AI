I'm Soumitra Deshpande, based in London. Soumitra.AI is where I publish my research and thinking on artificial intelligence — not the technology for its own sake, but what it means for the sectors, organisations and people who now have to make decisions about it.

## Where the perspective comes from

I came to strategy through engineering. My first degree was in electrical engineering, where I studied artificial intelligence and machine learning when they were still coursework rather than headlines — enough to understand how these systems actually work, and where the gap sits between what's claimed and what's real. I then read for an MSc in International Business at Warwick Business School, training in strategy analysis, economic behaviour and international markets.

Since then, my work has lived where analysis meets decisions. I've worked inside the CEO office of a £500M+ group through a live M&A and investor process — building board-level reporting across revenue, EBITDA and operational performance, consolidating group-wide risk, and analysing prospective investors for strategic fit. Before that, I worked across consulting engagements: market entry strategy spanning multiple verticals and geographies, competitive benchmarking, go-to-market design, and leading teams of consultants to deliver under real deadlines.

That combination — knowing how the machinery works *and* how boardrooms decide — is the lens for everything published here.

## What I believe

AI is not another technology cycle to be "adopted." It is the beginning of a new order in how value is created, how organisations are structured, and how sectors compete — and most commentary either oversells the moment or refuses to take it seriously. The interesting work is in between: figuring out, sector by sector and decision by decision, what actually changes, what doesn't, and when.

## Why this is in the open

Most analysis of this quality sits behind an engagement fee or a paywall. Publishing in the open imposes a useful discipline — every claim here carries my name, and you should expect rigour, independence, and honesty about uncertainty. Where I'm speculating, I'll say so.
